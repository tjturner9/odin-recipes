Man-Eating Plant Tart

Serves: 3–4
Flavour: Salty, hearty, and definitely not vegan

Ingredients

  • 200 ml leftover scorpion broth
  • 80 grams slime gelatin
  • 3 medium Baraselia (peeled and deseeded)
  • 5–6 medium Meeroak (peeled)
  • 3 medium Betan (peeled)
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper (a little)

Instructions

  1. Prep the Fruit Shell:
    Peel the Baraselia and reserve the peels. Flatten them and line a shallow tart pan. Set aside.
  2. Mash the Filling:
    Chop Meeroak and Betan. Mash some pieces, leave others chunky. Dice Baraselia flesh and mix in.
  3. Make the Slime-Scorpion Mixture:
    Warm the scorpion broth on low heat. Stir in slime gelatin until dissolved. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Combine & Fill:
    Mix the gelatin broth with the fruit. Spoon into the lined tart pan and smooth the top.
  5. Cook the Tart:
    Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 30–35 minutes until set and browned on top.
  6. Garnish & Serve:
    Top with fresh Meeroak or Baraselia slices. Serve warm or at room temp.
